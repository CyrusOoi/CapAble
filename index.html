Why We Build

The Problem

Millions of people who are deaf or hard of hearing rely on sign language as their primary means of communication. Yet the vast majority of the hearing world cannot understand it — creating a daily, invisible barrier that limits access to education, healthcare, employment, and social connection.

Existing solutions are either too expensive, too bulky, or too disconnected from how people actually communicate in the real world. CapAble aims to change that.

430M

people worldwide live with disabling hearing loss — over 5% of the global population.

What is CapAble?
Sign Language → Text · Real Time

Sign language,
translated instantly.

CapAble is a lightweight clip-on device that attaches to any cap. Using an onboard camera and machine learning, it reads hand gestures in real time and converts them into text — making communication effortless for everyone in the conversation.

👁️

Camera-based

ESP32-CAM captures sign language gestures hands-free.

🔤

Text Output

Gestures are converted to readable text in seconds.

🧢

Wearable

Origin Story

Our Past Prototype

Where it all began.

Version 1.0

The first step towards bridging the gap

Our initial prototype explored the core concept of wearable gesture recognition. Using basic hardware components and early machine learning models, we validated that a cap-mounted camera could reliably capture sign language gestures in everyday lighting conditions.

The experience taught us critical lessons about form factor, processing speed, and user comfort — all of which shaped the refined solution we have today.

Prototype Manufacturing

Built using lightweight PLA for comfort and durability.

The CapAble housing was engineered through multiple iterations of 3D printed prototypes. We used lightweight PLA filament to reduce overall weight while maintaining structural rigidity for daily wearable use.

Material PLA Lightweight Filament
Infill Gyroid Structure for strength and weight reduction
Focus Comfort, durability, and portability
